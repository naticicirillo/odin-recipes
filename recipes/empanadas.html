Empanadas

Description

Empanadas are savory, hand-held pastries filled with a delicious mixture of ground beef, onions, spices, and olives, all enclosed in a flaky pastry shell. These empanadas are a beloved classic in Argentine cuisine, known for their rich and hearty flavors. They make for a perfect snack, appetizer, or even a main course.

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) cold unsalted butter, diced
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup cold water

For the Filling:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
  • 1/2 cup green olives, pitted and chopped
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil (for sautéing)

Steps

For the Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. Add the cold, diced butter to the flour mixture. Using a pastry cutter or your fingers, work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  3. In a separate small bowl, beat the egg and cold water together.
  4. Gradually add the egg and water mixture to the flour mixture, mixing until the dough comes together.
  5. Shape the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate it for at least 30 minutes while you prepare the filling.

For the Filling:

  1. In a skillet, heat a bit of cooking oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add the ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks.
  4. Stir in the chopped hard-boiled eggs, chopped olives, raisins, ground cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until everything is well combined. Remove from heat and let it cool.

Assembling the Empanadas:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Roll out the chilled dough on a floured surface until it's about 1/8 inch thick.
  3. Use a round cutter or a small plate to cut out circles from the dough (about 5-6 inches in diameter).
  4. Place a spoonful of the meat mixture in the center of each dough circle.
  5. Fold the dough over the filling to create a half-moon shape and crimp the edges with a fork to seal.
  6. Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Brush the tops of the empanadas with a beaten egg for a golden finish.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown.
