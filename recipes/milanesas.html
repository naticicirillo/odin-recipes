Milanesas

Description

Milanesas are a cherished classic in Argentine cuisine. This dish features thinly sliced beef, breaded and fried to a crispy, golden perfection. With a tender interior and a flavorful, crispy coating, Milanesas are typically served with lemon wedges for that perfect zesty touch. Enjoyed by people of all ages, Milanesas make for a comforting and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 4 beef cutlets (thinly sliced, about 1/4 inch thick)
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Cooking oil (for frying)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Steps

  1. Set up a breading station with three shallow dishes: one with all-purpose flour, one with beaten eggs (seasoned with a pinch of salt and pepper) and one with breadcrumbs.
  2. Season the beef cutlets with salt and pepper.
  3. Dredge each cutlet in the flour, ensuring both sides are evenly coated.
  4. Dip the floured cutlet into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off.
  5. Coat the cutlet with breadcrumbs, pressing them onto both sides to adhere.
  6. In a large skillet, add enough cooking oil to submerge the cutlets halfway. Heat it over medium-high heat.
  7. Carefully place the breaded cutlets in the hot oil, ensuring they don't overlap or crowd the pan.
  8. Fry each side for about 3-4 minutes or until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  9. Transfer the cooked Milanesas to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.
  10. Serve the Milanesas hot, garnished with lemon wedges.
