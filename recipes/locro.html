Locro

Description

Locro is a hearty and comforting stew that has its roots in the indigenous cultures of Argentina. This dish is a celebration of the country's agricultural heritage, featuring a delicious blend of hominy corn, pork, and a medley of spices. It's traditionally enjoyed during national holidays and cold winter days, making it a beloved staple of Argentine cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups hominy corn (canned or dried and soaked overnight)
  • 1 lb (450g) pork shoulder, diced
  • 1/2 lb (225g) chorizo sausage, sliced
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro (for garnish)
  • Sliced green onions (for garnish)

Steps

  1. If you're using dried hominy corn, soak it in water overnight, then drain and rinse it thoroughly.
  2. If you have canned hominy corn, simply rinse and drain it. Set aside.
  3. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat a bit of cooking oil over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the diced pork shoulder and sliced chorizo sausage to the pot.
  5. Cook until the pork is browned and the chorizo is slightly crispy. Remove them from the pot and set aside.
  6. In the same pot, add the finely chopped onion and minced garlic.
  7. Sauté them until the onion becomes translucent and fragrant, usually for about 3-5 minutes.
  8. To the sautéed aromatics, add the paprika, ground cumin, dried oregano, cayenne pepper (adjust to your preferred level of spiciness), and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  9. Stir in the hominy corn to combine it with the spices and aromatics.
  10. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth into the pot, then add back the browned pork and chorizo.
  11. Mix everything thoroughly and bring it to a boil.
  12. Once it's boiling, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld, and the hominy corn should become tender.
  13. Stir in the heavy cream and let it simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes. This will thicken the stew slightly and add a creamy richness.
  14. Ladle the hot Locro into bowls.
  15. Garnish each bowl with chopped fresh cilantro and sliced green onions.
